AI methods statement

Which parts of this edition are machine-generated, which are human-reviewed, and where the pipelines can go wrong. Version 2026-07-05.

1. Text extraction

The source PDF (Babken Chookaszian, Collected Works Vol. I, Yerevan 2022/2023, 2nd expanded edition) carries a full text layer. We extract it with pdfminer.six and do not re-OCR any page. Form-feed characters preserved by pdfminer become <pb n="P"/> milestones at every printed-page boundary. Any LLM-vs-extraction conflict resolves in favour of the extraction unless a human editor overrules it.

2. TEI encoding + validation

Every article is TEI P5 4.11.0. Both layers of validation run on every push:

  • RelaxNG against tei_all.rng from tei-c.org — base structural conformance.
  • ISO Schematron against odd/chookaszian.sch — project-specific rules: teiHeader completeness, xml:id on standoff persons and manuscripts, @n required on every <pb/>, @xml:lang and @type allow-list warnings.

CI blocks merge on any RelaxNG or Schematron error. Warnings are visible but do not block, so we can tighten the vocabulary iteratively.

3. Entity enrichment (persons + manuscripts)

pipeline/enrich_tei.py extracts likely person names and manuscript shelfmarks with a rule-based heuristic: NFC-normalised Armenian regex candidates, a curated stopword list, inflectional deduplication, and title/genitive filters. Output goes into the standoff register.

Every extracted entity is auto-generated and marked as such. Person and manuscript entries carry a resp="auto" attribute; the article-page entity panel renders a chip that reads auto (unreviewed). Do not cite an auto-only entity as gospel — spot-check against the article text (link to the manuscript catalogue is provided where a Wikidata/Matenadaran identifier exists).

QA reports for the 001–055 range live at eval/. Known limits: 28 of 55 articles yield zero persons (lexicon-style entries); article 054 dropped 2 bibliographic-title false positives after the Vol. I second-pass tightening.

4. Retrieval and citation ("Ask the corpus")

The retrieval index is intentionally hybrid:

  • BM25 over lemmatised chunks with a transliteration table (Armenian → Latin for cross-language surface matching)
  • BGE-M3 dense embeddings stored in ChromaDB (multilingual, closes the gap on English queries against the Armenian corpus)
  • Fused with Reciprocal Rank Fusion (k=60)

Answer generation uses Anthropic's Citations API. Every text block in the response must carry at least one citation to a retrieved chunk; if any span is uncited the verification gate marks the answer unverified, and strict-mode callers get an abstention instead of the answer.

Abstention is visible. When the retrieval score is below threshold or when the verification gate rejects, the response is literally: “The corpus does not contain sufficiently relevant information to answer this question reliably.” There is no fallback to model-only knowledge.

Retrieval eval sets and citation-accuracy measurements are published in rag/eval/.

5. IIIF plates and manuscript register

The plates insert (23 pages, pp. 549–571) is delivered as static IIIF Image API 3.0 tiles with a Presentation 3.0 manifest. Ten miniatures are Matenadaran holdings; each has a <msDesc> with xml:id in a corpus-level register (tei/standoff_plates_manuscripts.xml), and the manifest carries per-canvas seeAlso links pointing at that TEI entry. IIIF Content State URLs are the canonical citation format for a specific plate region.

Plate tiles are not published until the Matenadaran per-image clearance and the heirs' licence are signed. The manifest ships with rights: InC-RUW and a metadata field Public visibility: pending rights clearance.

6. What we do NOT do

  • We do not normalise classical (Mesropian) to reformed orthography, or vice versa. Both are preserved as printed.
  • We do not present a "Chookaszian persona" chatbot. The retrieval endpoint answers with citations to his articles; it does not impersonate him.
  • We do not generate free abstractive summaries. If a summary appears on a page, it is either printed source material or a labelled human editorial note.
  • We do not ship translations without a two-tier label. "AI draft, unrevised" and "reviewed by [scholar]" are never blended. Currently no translations are published.

7. Provenance and version

Every artefact is regenerated from source by CI on every push (workflows). The corpus manifest lives at tei/corpus_manifest.json and records the extractor version, enrichment scope, and known open tickets. Each article file preserves its <publicationStmt> and <sourceDesc>.
